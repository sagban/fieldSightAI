API 510 - Pressure Vessel Inspection Code: In-Service Inspection, Rating, Repair, and Alteration
(Representative Excerpts for Engineering Reference)

=== Section 5.2 - Corrosion Rate Determination ===

5.2.1 Short-Term Corrosion Rate
The short-term corrosion rate shall be calculated using the most recent two thickness measurements:

  Corrosion Rate (mm/year) = (t_previous - t_current) / Δt

where:
  t_previous = wall thickness at prior inspection (mm)
  t_current  = wall thickness at current inspection (mm)
  Δt         = elapsed time between inspections (years)

5.2.2 Long-Term Corrosion Rate
The long-term rate uses the original nominal thickness and the most recent reading:

  Long-Term Rate = (t_nominal - t_current) / total_service_years

The more conservative (higher) of the short-term and long-term rates should be used for
remaining life calculations unless engineering judgment, supported by process data, justifies
use of the short-term rate.

=== Section 5.4 - Remaining Life Calculation ===

5.4.1 The remaining life of a vessel component shall be calculated as:

  Remaining Life (years) = (t_current - t_min) / Corrosion Rate

where t_min is the minimum allowable thickness per the original design code (ASME VIII Div.1).

5.4.2 If the calculated remaining life is less than or equal to zero, the component has
exceeded its safe operating envelope and IMMEDIATE action is required per Section 7.4.

5.4.3 If remaining life is less than the desired interval to next inspection (typically 5 years),
the inspection interval must be reduced, or repairs/replacements must be scheduled.

=== Section 5.6 - Evaluation of Inspection Findings ===

5.6.1 General Wall Thinning
When wall thickness surveys reveal uniform thinning, the minimum measured thickness shall be
compared against t_min. If t_measured < t_min, the vessel shall be:
  a) Removed from service for repair, OR
  b) Re-rated (de-rated) to a lower MAWP, OR
  c) A fitness-for-service (FFS) evaluation per API 579 shall be performed.

5.6.2 Localized Corrosion
5.6.2.1 Isolated pitting that does not exceed the corrosion allowance may be accepted for
continued service with enhanced monitoring (reduced inspection intervals).

5.6.2.2 When the maximum pit depth exceeds 50% of the nominal wall thickness, a fitness-for-
service evaluation per API 579 is recommended.

5.6.2.3 When pit depth exceeds the corrosion allowance, the pitted area shall be:
  a) Ground smooth and weld-overlaid, OR
  b) The component shall be replaced.
  Pitting in sour (H2S) service requires additional evaluation per NACE MR0175.

5.6.3 Cracking
All crack-like indications detected during inspection shall be evaluated as follows:
  a) Immediate removal from service if the crack is through-wall or propagating.
  b) NDT characterization of crack length, depth, and orientation.
  c) Cracks in pressure-containing components represent a risk of fast-fracture failure
     and are classified as Category A (Critical) findings requiring immediate response.

=== Section 6.4 - Inspection Intervals ===

6.4.1 The maximum inspection interval shall be the lesser of:
  a) One-half the calculated remaining life, OR
  b) 10 years for vessels in non-corrosive service, OR
  c) 5 years for vessels in corrosive or sour service.

6.4.2 For vessels with a remaining life less than 4 years, annual inspections are required.

=== Section 7.3 - Conditions for Continued Operation ===

7.3.1 A vessel may continue to operate with wall thickness below the original corrosion
allowance if:
  a) A fitness-for-service evaluation (API 579) confirms acceptability, AND
  b) An enhanced monitoring program is implemented, AND
  c) The inspection interval is reduced to no more than one-half the remaining life.

=== Section 7.4 - Repairs and Alterations ===

7.4.1 All repairs shall comply with the original construction code (ASME VIII) and this
inspection code.

7.4.2 Weld repairs on pressure-containing components shall be:
  a) Performed by qualified welders per ASME IX
  b) Subjected to PWHT where required by the construction code
  c) Inspected by NDE methods appropriate to the repair type

7.4.3 Shell section replacement is required when:
  a) Wall thickness is below t_min over an area exceeding 100 cm², OR
  b) Multiple crack-like indications are found in the same weld seam.

=== Section 5.5 - Service-Specific Concerns ===

5.5.1 Sour Service (H2S)
Vessels in sour service (as defined by NACE MR0175) are susceptible to:
  - Sulfide Stress Cracking (SSC)
  - Hydrogen-Induced Cracking (HIC)
  - Stress-Oriented Hydrogen-Induced Cracking (SOHIC)

A 20% safety factor shall be applied to all corrosion-rate-based remaining life
calculations for vessels in sour service. Pitting in sour service, even when within the
corrosion allowance, shall be classified as Category B (Warning) due to the risk of
hydrogen-assisted crack initiation at pit bottoms.
