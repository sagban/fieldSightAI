DNV-RP-C101 - Design of Offshore Steel Structures: Corrosion Protection
(Representative Excerpts for Engineering Reference)

=== Section 4 - Corrosion Allowances ===

4.1 General
Corrosion allowances shall be specified in the design basis for all structural and
pressure-containing components based on the expected corrosion environment.

4.2 Recommended Corrosion Allowances for FPSO Components:

  Component Type                  | Service           | Allowance (mm)
  ----------------------------------------------------------------
  Pressure Vessel (internal)      | Sweet crude        | 1.5 - 3.0
  Pressure Vessel (internal)      | Sour crude (H2S)   | 3.0 - 6.0
  Storage Tank (internal)         | Produced water      | 2.0 - 4.0
  Storage Tank (internal)         | Ballast water       | 1.5 - 3.0
  Heat Exchanger (shell side)     | Process gas         | 1.0 - 2.0
  Piping (process)                | Hydrocarbon         | 1.5 - 3.0

4.3 When measured corrosion exceeds the design corrosion allowance, the component
shall be assessed for fitness-for-service per API 579 or replaced.

=== Section 5 - Cathodic Protection ===

5.1 All submerged and buried steel components shall be protected by cathodic protection
(CP) systems, either sacrificial anode or impressed current.

5.2 The CP system design shall achieve a protection potential of -0.80V to -1.10V
(Ag/AgCl reference) on all protected surfaces.

5.3 CP systems shall be surveyed annually to verify adequate protection levels.

=== Section 6 - Coating Maintenance ===

6.1 Coating Systems
All atmospheric zone steel surfaces shall be protected with a qualified coating system.
The coating system selection shall consider:
  - Operating temperature range
  - Chemical exposure (H2S, CO2, chlorides)
  - UV exposure
  - Mechanical damage risk

6.2 Coating Inspection and Maintenance Schedule

  Coating Grade (ISO 4628) | Action Required
  --------------------------------------------------------
  Grade 0-2                | Routine monitoring (annual visual)
  Grade 3                  | Schedule spot repair at next maintenance window
  Grade 4                  | Full re-coat within 60 days. Implement enhanced UT monitoring.
  Grade 5                  | IMMEDIATE re-coat. Consider de-rating if wall loss detected.

6.3 Coating Repair Procedures
  a) Surface preparation to Sa 2½ (ISO 8501-1)
  b) Application of full coating system (primer + intermediate + topcoat)
  c) DFT measurement to verify minimum film thickness
  d) Cure time verification before return to service

=== Section 7 - Inspection Planning ===

7.1 Risk-Based Inspection (RBI) methodology should be applied to optimize inspection
resources. Components with the following characteristics require higher inspection priority:
  - Sour service (H2S environment)
  - High operating temperature (> 100°C)
  - Previous corrosion findings exceeding 50% of corrosion allowance
  - Coating Grade 3 or above

7.2 Inspection intervals shall not exceed:
  - 5 years for components in corrosive service
  - 10 years for components in non-corrosive service with effective CP and coating
  - 1 year for components with remaining life less than 4 years
