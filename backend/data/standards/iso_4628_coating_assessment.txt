ISO 4628 - Paints and Varnishes: Evaluation of Degradation of Coatings
(Representative Excerpts for Engineering Reference)

=== Section 1 - Scope ===

This standard specifies methods for the assessment of coating degradation on steel
substrates, including blistering, rusting, cracking, flaking, and chalking. It provides
a numerical grading system (Grades 0-5) for each type of degradation.

=== Section 3 - Coating Condition Grading System ===

3.1 Grade 0 - No detectable degradation
    Coating is intact with no visible defects. Full protection maintained.

3.2 Grade 1 - Very minor degradation
    Isolated spots of degradation affecting less than 0.05% of the coated area.
    ACTION: No action required. Standard monitoring schedule.

3.3 Grade 2 - Minor degradation
    Scattered degradation affecting 0.05% to 0.5% of the coated area.
    ACTION: Monitor at next scheduled inspection. No immediate re-coating needed.

3.4 Grade 3 - Moderate degradation
    Noticeable degradation affecting 0.5% to 1% of the coated area.
    Substrate may be partially exposed in isolated areas.
    ACTION: Schedule spot re-coating at next available maintenance window.
    This grade is a WARNING indicator -- degradation is accelerating.

3.5 Grade 4 - Significant degradation
    Substantial degradation affecting 1% to 8% of the coated area.
    Significant substrate exposure with active corrosion likely.
    ACTION: COATING SYSTEM HAS FAILED. Full surface preparation and re-coat required
    within 60 days. Enhanced UT monitoring of uncoated areas.
    Reference: DNV-RP-C101 Section 6 for offshore coating maintenance requirements.

3.6 Grade 5 - Severe degradation
    Extensive degradation affecting more than 8% of the coated area.
    Coating system is non-functional. Rapid corrosion of exposed substrate expected.
    ACTION: IMMEDIATE re-coating required. De-rate vessel if wall thinning detected.

=== Section 4 - Assessment of Coating Degradation ===

4.1 Assessment shall be performed visually by qualified inspectors.

4.2 The highest grade of degradation observed in any single inspection zone shall be
reported as the overall coating condition grade for that component.

4.3 Photographic documentation is required for Grade 3 and above.

4.4 For offshore structures (FPSO, platform topsides, subsea), coating assessment shall
also consider:
  - Marine growth coverage (which masks underlying coating degradation)
  - Splash zone exposure (accelerated degradation due to wet-dry cycling)
  - Proximity to hot surfaces or chemical exposure points

=== Section 5 - Relationship to Corrosion Rates ===

5.1 Coating failure (Grade 4-5) significantly accelerates corrosion rates. The typical
corrosion rate for bare carbon steel in a marine environment is 0.1-0.3 mm/year, but
this can increase to 0.5-1.0 mm/year in splash zones with failed coatings.

5.2 When coating failure is detected, the inspection interval for UT thickness surveys
should be reduced to ensure the increased corrosion rate is captured in remaining life
calculations.
