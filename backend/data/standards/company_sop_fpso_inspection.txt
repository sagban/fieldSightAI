FieldSight Energy - Standard Operating Procedure: FPSO Asset Integrity Inspection
Document No: FS-SOP-AI-001 | Rev: 3 | Effective: 2025-01-01

=== 1. Purpose ===

This SOP defines the procedures for conducting integrity inspections of pressure vessels,
storage tanks, and heat exchangers on FieldSight Energy FPSO units. It supplements the
requirements of API 510, ISO 4628, and DNV-RP-C101 with company-specific procedures.

=== 2. Scope ===

Applies to all pressure-containing and structural components on FPSO vessels operated by
FieldSight Energy, including:
  - 1st and 2nd stage separators
  - Produced water treatment vessels
  - Cargo and slop tanks
  - Shell-and-tube heat exchangers
  - Process piping (NPS 2" and above)

=== 3. Inspection Workflow ===

3.1 Pre-Inspection
  a) Obtain work permit and confirm safe access
  b) Select asset from the approved Asset Register (source of truth)
  c) Review previous inspection reports and trending data
  d) Verify environmental conditions (temperature, humidity, sea state)

3.2 Asset Verification
  a) Visually confirm the asset nameplate matches the selected Asset ID
  b) Record nameplate data: manufacturer, serial number, MAWP, design code
  c) Photograph the nameplate for audit trail

3.3 Data Collection (in order)
  a) Ultrasonic Thickness (UT):
     - Minimum 4 readings per inspection zone
     - Record average and minimum thickness
     - Use calibrated equipment (± 0.1mm accuracy)
  b) Pitting Assessment:
     - Pit gauge measurements for deepest pit in each zone
     - Record maximum pit depth
  c) Coating Assessment:
     - Grade per ISO 4628 (1-5 scale)
     - Note areas of blistering, flaking, or chalking
  d) Crack Detection:
     - Visual inspection of all weld seams
     - MPI or TOFD for suspect areas
     - Report any crack-like indications

3.4 Data Review and Categorization
  All findings shall be categorized using the FieldSight 4-tier system:

  Category A - CRITICAL
    Triggers: Cracks detected, wall thickness below t_min, remaining life ≤ 0
    Response: IMMEDIATE notification to Integrity Manager and OIM.
              Asset must be isolated within 4 hours.
              Emergency repair plan within 24 hours.
    Escalation: Mandatory notification to regulatory authority within 48 hours.

  Category B - WARNING
    Triggers: Pitting exceeds corrosion allowance, coating Grade 4+, sour service concerns
    Response: Formal work order within 7 days.
              Repair/mitigation within 60 days.
              Enhanced monitoring until resolved.

  Category C - MONITOR
    Triggers: Coating Grade 3, minor pitting within allowance, trending concerns
    Response: Document in inspection report.
              Reduce inspection interval if trending adversely.
              Schedule maintenance at next turnaround.

  Normal - ACCEPTABLE
    Triggers: All metrics within design limits
    Response: Standard inspection interval continues.
              Update trending database.

=== 4. Safety Escalation Matrix ===

4.1 Category A findings trigger the following mandatory actions:
  - Immediate verbal notification to the Offshore Installation Manager (OIM)
  - Written notification to the Integrity Engineering Manager within 1 hour
  - The inspector MUST NOT dismiss or downgrade a Category A finding without
    written approval from a Level III inspector and the Integrity Manager
  - All Category A findings require an independent verification inspection
    within 7 days

4.2 Sour Service Protocol
  For all assets in H2S service:
  - Apply 20% safety factor to remaining life calculations
  - Any pitting > 2.0mm depth is automatically Category B regardless of
    corrosion allowance
  - Annual HIC/SSC screening is mandatory

=== 5. Reporting ===

5.1 All inspection findings shall be recorded in the FieldSight CMMS within 24 hours.
5.2 Reports must include: asset ID, location, all measurements, category, prescribed
    action, standard references, inspector ID, and approval status.
5.3 Category A reports require sign-off by:
    a) The inspecting engineer
    b) The Integrity Engineering Manager
    c) The OIM (for operational decisions)
